ICP RIE

Process Description: 

  • Inductively coupled reactive ion etching (ICP RIE) is an advanced technique designed to deliver high etch rates, high selectivity and low damage processing. 

  • Excellent profile control is also provided as the plasma can be maintained at low pressures. 

  • The Cobra® ICP sources produce a uniform, high density plasma with the capability to operate at low pressures. 

  • Substrate DC bias is independently controlled by a separate RF generator, allowing control of ion energy giving excellent results tailored to specific process requirements.

Process Description: 

  • Vacuum pump: Turbo pumping system keeps RIE processes operating under vacuum.

  • Etching chamber: Plasma is generated using a two-step process. Initially, a separate radiofrequency power source creates an inductively coupled plasma (ICP) in a separate chamber, which is then introduced into the etching chamber. This high-density plasma is then coupled with a lower-frequency RF power to create a more controlled and energetic plasma towards the sample for etching.

  • Laser interferometry: Precisely measures etch depth within a layer in situ using interferometric detection. Precise etch depth control within multi-layer structure is possible.

  • Emergency stop button: In case of emergency, push the emergency stop button. If pressed, the system operation will halt immediately.

 

Operation Procedure:

  1. Opening the software on PC
  2. Verify system status
  3. Run pre-clean and conditioning recipe
  4. Vent the chamber
  5. When the loadlock is fully vented, open the chamber by turning the switch, then press the 2 green button at the same time and hold until the chamber rises and rotates 90 degrees.
  6. Load samples
  7. Close the chamber
  8. Return to computer screen, go to the recipe screen to edit the recipe for etching process (e.g., gases, flow, pressure, time, ...)
  9. Run the desired recipe: click ‘run’ to start the process
  10. After finishing the etching process, vent and open the chamber, then take out your sample.
  11. Pump down the chamber after use.

 

Safety Warnings: 

  • Always leave the chamber in vacuum state.
  • Adhere to the list of allowed materials that can be introduced to ICP-RIE
  • Push the emergency stop button in case of emergency.
